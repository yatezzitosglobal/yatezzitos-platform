Cheap Can Be Costly: Identify Prices That Are Just a Hook   

We know that price is one of the most important factors when purchasing any product or service. However, you should know that in the car rental industry, there are companies that advertise ridiculously low prices that are just a hook.   

Their strategy involves charging you a much higher amount when providing the service than what they initially offered. For example, if you compare car rental Guadalajara Airport prices on specialized websites, you’ll notice that, for vehicles in the same category, there’s a significant variation in rates between companies. This is because some don’t include the cost of mandatory insurance in their initial price. Therefore, when picking up the car, you’ll have to pay for this additional insurance without exception. 

This is why, when choosing based on price, it’s important to ensure you’re comparing the final price and not just the initial price that hooks you. Later, we’ll discuss in more detail the aspects you should ensure are included in the car rental price.   

Understand the Rental Conditions   

All services come with conditions, but the reality is that we rarely take the time to carefully read the terms we agree to when signing a contract. Despite knowing the importance of reviewing the contract thoroughly, we often sign without reading. That’s why we want to tell you about the most important points typically included in these contracts so that, before signing, you can ask questions and ensure there are no unfavorable clauses for you. And if there are, and they can’t be modified, at least you’ll be aware of what you’re committing yourself to.   

  • Stick to the Reserved Schedule: While picking up the car 30 minutes late is usually not a problem, returning it 30 minutes late can result in a penalty, especially if the company has already booked the vehicle for another customer.   
  • Cancellation Policies: Car rentals and other tourist services are considered “perishable.” This means that, for example, an airline can’t sell a seat today that was empty on yesterday’s flight. For this reason, contracts often include specific cancellation clauses. Our advice is to ask directly what happens if you need to cancel the service. Find out if there’s a period where you can cancel without penalties or what happens if the cancellation is due to natural causes, like a hurricane.   
  • Deposits: In car rentals, it’s common to require a deposit. These deposits serve as a guarantee for potential damages. Before booking, check if a deposit is required and its amount. Especially in Guadalajara, some unethical rental companies arbitrarily increase the deposit amount as a way to pressure customers into purchasing additional insurance.   

Beware of Hidden Fees : Car Rental Guadalajara

Earlier, we mentioned that in the car rental industry, there are dishonest companies that use hidden fees to charge customers more. In this section, we’ll tell you about the most common surprise fees so that, when booking, you can ensure they’re already included in the price.   

Common Hidden Fees in Car Rentals  

  • Car Protection Insurance: By far the most common hidden fee. Don’t count on being able to use the insurance provided by your credit card. The problem isn’t that it’s invalid, but if you booked a “hook” price, the company will rely on generating additional income by selling its own insurance. This is often sold at a much higher price than the rental itself, increasing the total cost up to 5 times the initial amount.   
  • Airport Surcharges or “Premium Facilities”: Airport branches often add a 19% airport tax, which isn’t always included in the initial price. Although this fee is imposed by the airport, it’s often not clarified when booking, simply stating “plus taxes.” This leads many customers to assume they’ll only pay 16% VAT, when in reality, adding both rates, the total increase is 35%. Even outside the airport, some companies apply a similar charge for “premium facilities,” even if these facilities are nothing special. This type of fee is, in reality, a strategy to generate additional income rather than offering a lower price in locations not subject to airport taxes.   
  • Unfavorable Exchange Rate: It’s not unusual to receive a quote that seems like a good price in dollars. The trap is that, at the time of payment, the rental company applies an exchange rate much higher than the official one. Since car rental transactions are almost exclusively done via credit card, the customer has no option to pay in cash to avoid the unfavorable exchange rate.   

Tips for Choosing the Best Car Rental Guadalajara 

  • Know What You Need: When renting a car, be clear about how many people will be traveling, how much luggage you’ll have, and where you want to pick up and drop off the car. Having answers to these questions will help you choose the right car and location.   
  • Choose Companies That Honor Reservations: Particularly in car rentals, it’s common for traditional rental companies in high-demand offices, like those at the Guadalajara Airport, to accept multiple reservations for the same car. The intention behind this is to prioritize the reservation with the highest rate or longest duration, leaving other customers without a car or with a different vehicle than the one reserved. The worst part is that if the available car is smaller, they usually don’t offer a discount, but if it’s larger, they’ll charge you the price difference.   
  • Book in Advance: In the tourism industry, booking in advance usually means getting better rates. Additionally, this allows you to choose from more car options, which may no longer be available if you wait too long.   

How to Choose the Perfect Car Rental Guadalajara

  • Research Any Additional Fees: When comparing Car Rental Guadalajara options, choose companies that don’t add extra charges. Contact each rental company to confirm this. If there are additional fees, factor them into the total cost before making a decision. 
  • Read Reviews and Check the Company’s Reputation: It’s almost certain that if a company uses dishonest strategies like last-minute additional fees, other customers will have written about it online. The information you need to make a good decision is already available on sites like Google Maps, Yelp, Tripadvisor, and others.   
  • Avoid Intermediaries: It’s always recommended to book directly with the operating company. Neither Kayak, Expedia, nor Booking have their own fleets of rental cars in Guadalajara or anywhere else; they are just intermediaries. In case of a problem with the rental company, they don’t have an in-person representative to help you resolve it, leaving you on your own. In the best-case scenario, if the service isn’t provided, they’ll offer a refund, which could take months.
